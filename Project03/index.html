Chapter 1: The Beginning

The sun floated over Oxford, struggling to permeate through the thick clouds. The half-naked trees danced to the cold gusts of wind that herald the coming of winter. It was gloomy. Miami students, though accustomed to the seasons of Oxford, felt something was off. The sentiments of the student body, echoing from classrooms to bars Uptown, were confirmed when Prsident Crawford announced a disturbing news later that evening.

Chapter 2: The Evening

The moon now dominated the sky of Oxford. The trees, now stripped from their leaves, stood still. It was a quiet night. Everyone was wondering what the President has to say as they wait around the hastily erected podium. The President, wearing a hazmat suit and surrounded by Oxford police officers, rushed through the crowd and onto the podium. He speaks into the mic: "I gathered you all here today to make an urgent announcement. Five Biology students at Miami have been infected by a human-engineered intentionally fast-spreading and deadly virus that broke out from a lab in Upham. No one will be able to enter and leave Oxford as per a recently instated emergency movement ban by the CDC." Students started looking at each other in shock. "Love and Honor," said the President as his closing statement before he rushes away from the podium.

Chapter 3: The End

Oxford has become the center of attention for much of the world. The town has been surrounded by the U.S army and a federally erected barrier. Given the risk of the virus escaping the Oxford bubble, the CDC has announced that it will be exerising a "non-interventionist approach." The federal government will not allow the movement of anyone within the barrier: "those who were outside of Oxford ought to remain so and those inside ought to remain inside. The city of Oxford has been left to fight on its own. Within days, everyone in Oxford contracts the virus, and everyone eventually is declared dead. The federal government authorizes the complete "biological and physical" eradication of the city, and the city becomes permanently blocked from the rest of the world.
